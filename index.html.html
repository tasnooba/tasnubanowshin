Motivation

The 2022 launch of Dhaka MRT Line-6 created an opportunity to study what drives commuters to shift from private vehicles and rickshaws to mass rapid transit — with direct implications for equitable transit policy across megacities in the Global South.

Methodology

A stratified multi-channel survey instrument (43 variables) was administered across metro stations, roadside intercepts, workplaces, and online platforms. Four classifiers (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost) were trained on SMOTE-balanced data and evaluated via cross-validation.

Key Finding

Travel time savings dominated switching decisions at mean |SHAP| = 0.165 — approximately 3.2× the magnitude of secondary features. Critically, demographic variables (income, gender, age) exerted negligible predictive influence (SHAP < 0.05), challenging the income-mediated shift paradigm and indicating that service performance is the primary driver of equitable metro adoption regardless of socioeconomic background.

Projects

Microsimulation-Based Traffic Intervention Study
Transportation Engineering Laboratory, AUST · 2025 · Tejgaon Road, Dhaka

Led a team to conduct field data collection and PTV VISSIM microsimulation modelling at one of Dhaka's most congested arterials, evaluating the traffic-level impacts of three distinct engineering interventions.

  • Captured real-time traffic volumes and vehicle speeds through a structured field protocol at a high-congestion intersection.
  • Calibrated a PTV VISSIM model to observed conditions before scenario testing to ensure validity.
  • Signal retiming reduced intersection delay; dedicated bus lanes improved transit throughput; pedestrian-exclusive phasing improved safety but required complementary lane enforcement to preserve capacity gains.
